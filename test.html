Stephanie & Matthew

oppedette | France 

 

There are countless voyages one can take to reach the small village of Oppedette, in the stony landscape of Provence in southeastern France. The one I took involved rising before the sun in Dublin city, catching a plane with clover logos on the wings, arriving in Marseille, witnessing a huge traffic jam created by angry taxi drivers on strike and driving north through the hilly lavender fields for about 2 hours. Did I mention we stopped for some beverages on the way to start the celebration early?

The gathering of Stephanie & Matthew went over many days. We watched the sun come up and go down, sometimes because there was no sleep in between. Bike riding on Mount Ventoux resulted in my legs wedging the rest of my body into place in the boot of a speeding Jeep, just to photograph a moment that was not possible from any other angle.

Champagne exploded by accident onto the backs of friends during the ceremony because some of the lads thought it felt nice to rub the chilled bottles on their necks and faces to cool them a little. And finally the ‘deep and meaningful’ sessions you inevitably have during the early hours of the morning, ‘You know you’re an alright guy Shan-ta-nu, I really dig what you do, it’s special, whenever you come to Geneva, come and stay with us and we’ll show you a good time’.

I left southeastern France with about fifty more people in my family, some wine and a slightly sore head, which I’m sure was a result from four days of smelling too many fresh lavender plants.
